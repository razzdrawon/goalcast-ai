## Venue Segments — Read this before pitching

Not every venue has the same problem. Identify the segment first, then use the right pitch.

---

### Segment A — High-traffic sports bar (near stadium, already famous)
**Examples:** Brewhouse Cafe (new location near Mercedes-Benz), STATS Brewpub, Fado Irish Pub
**Their reality:** They will be packed during every match regardless. Traffic is not a pain point.
**What they actually need:** Know who those fans are. Keep them after the tournament ends.

**Opening:**
"The World Cup is going to bring you fans who've never walked in before. GoalCast tells you who they are and gives you a reason for them to come back in August."

**Pitch focus:**
- Fan profile data per match night — which teams' fans are coming, where they're from
- Post-tournament CRM: a database of soccer fans who came specifically during the World Cup, that they can reactivate for MLS, Champions League, Copa América
- The leaderboard with their name circulates on social for 5 weeks — organic reach they don't have to pay for
- Founding venue placement: even when we add 50 more venues, they stay at the top

**Don't mention:** driving traffic. They'll tune out immediately.

---

### Segment B — Mid-tier sports bar with soccer culture
**Examples:** The Midway Pub, Ashford Lane, bars in Decatur WatchFest
**Their reality:** Busy on big matches (Spain, Mexico, USA), quiet on smaller group stage games.
**What they actually need:** Consistent foot traffic across all 104 matches, not just the headliners.

**Opening:**
"Spain vs Saudi Arabia is going to fill you up either way. We're talking about Congo vs Uzbekistan on a Tuesday — that's where GoalCast keeps the fans engaged and gives them a reason to show up."

**Pitch focus:**
- GoalCast fans are committed to all 104 matches, not just the big ones — they have predictions on every game
- Trivia during the match keeps them at the bar for the full 90 minutes
- Prize per match gives fans a reason to show up even for low-profile games
- Pre-match report so they can staff and stock correctly for each specific game

---

### Segment C — Latino restaurant / neighborhood bar
**Examples:** Buford Highway corridor, Chamblee spots, barrios en Roma/Condesa CDMX
**Their reality:** Strong existing community of regulars who already watch soccer there. No formal watch party structure.
**What they actually need:** Give their regulars something extra to do during the match. Deepen existing loyalty.

**Opening:**
"Your regulars are already going to be here for every Mexico game. GoalCast gives them a competition to play while they watch — and a reason to bring friends who don't usually come."

**Pitch focus:**
- GoalCast adds a game layer on top of what they're already doing — no setup needed from them
- The prize dynamic is natural: "best predictor of the week gets a free meal" — they've probably done versions of this informally already
- Bilingual app (Spanish/English) fits their community
- Friends of regulars who pick this venue in the app become new customers

---

### Segment D — After-party venue (near stadium, doesn't show the match)
**Examples:** Late-night bars, lounges, restaurants that close late near Mercedes-Benz Stadium
**Their reality:** 69,000 fans exit the stadium after each match. Those fans are hyped, hungry and looking for somewhere to go. These venues can capture that crowd without competing with sports bars during the game.
**What they actually need:** Be the obvious next stop after the match.

**Opening:**
"You don't need to show the game. You need to be where fans go after. GoalCast sends a push notification to every fan at the final whistle — 'Spain won, head to [Venue] for the after-party, check your leaderboard there.'"

**Pitch focus:**
- Official After-Party Venue status in the app — different category, no competition with match-day sports bars
- Push notification at final whistle drives fans directly to them while the energy is still high
- Leaderboard check after the game = reason to open the app at their venue
- Prize announcement after the match = fans want to see who won while they celebrate

**This is a completely different conversation** — they're not competing for the match crowd, they're capturing the post-match crowd that no one else is targeting.

---

### Segment E — Restaurant wanting to do watch parties but starting from zero
**Examples:** Any restaurant that sees the opportunity but has no sports bar infrastructure
**Their reality:** They want in but don't know how to structure it. Every detail feels like work.
**What they actually need:** A ready-made watch party product they can plug in.

**Opening:**
"You don't need to build a watch party from scratch. GoalCast is the structure — predictions, trivia, leaderboard, prizes. You put up the screens and the drinks. We bring the format."

**Pitch focus:**
- GoalCast handles all the engagement mechanics — they just need to show up and serve
- Pre-match fan count report tells them how to staff and prep
- Prize structure gives the night a narrative without them having to invent one
- Founding venue status gives them credibility from day one even without history as a soccer bar

## Restaurant Pitch (in person — generic, adapt per segment above)

### What to bring
- Phone with demo open at goalcastai.com/demo
- Business card or note with goalcastai.com

### Opening line
Adapt per segment. The common thread: lead with what matters to them specifically, not with what GoalCast does.

### The 3-minute pitch

**1. Show the demo** (goalcastai.com/demo)
"This is what fans see. They predict every match, get AI analysis before each game, compete on a leaderboard, and play live trivia during the match. The leaderboard shows which venue they're watching from — your name is on it."

**2. Show the venue benefit** (pick the angle for their segment)
Segment A: "The World Cup brings you new fans. This tells you who they are and keeps them coming back."
Segment B: "This keeps fans showing up for every match, not just the big ones."
Segment C: "Your regulars now have a competition running all tournament — and they bring friends."
Segment D: "We send fans to your door the second the final whistle blows."
Segment E: "The format is ready. You just add the screens and the drinks."

**3. The ask**
"Two things: your name and logo in the app, and you define a prize for your customers — whatever makes sense for you. Could be a round of drinks per match, something weekly, something at the end. You set the rules. We announce the winner with your name on it."

**4. Close**
"The first match is June 15. I'm confirming founding venues this week — first ones in have permanent top placement. Are you in?"

## Common Objections & Responses

**"We don't know if we'll have watch parties yet"**
"That's exactly why this helps — when fans start selecting your venue in the app, you'll see weeks in advance how many people to expect. It helps you decide whether to invest in watch party setup or not. The data comes to you before you commit."

**"How many users do you have?"**
"We're launching now with venues as the first step. Fans sign up when they see official venues listed — you being in is what makes fans join. I need a few venues to open the app to the public. You'd be one of the founding venues, which means you get the most visibility when fans first open the app."

**"We don't want to spend money right now"**
"There's no money to me — nothing. The only cost is whatever prize you decide to give your own customers. Could be a free appetizer per match, a round of drinks weekly, or a dinner at the end of the tournament. You set the rules. And that prize goes to a customer who's been coming to your bar all tournament — it's a loyalty tool disguised as a prize."

**"What do you get out of this?"**
"The app is free for basic use — predictions, leaderboard, trivia. I offer a paid version at $9.99 for the full tournament with deeper AI analysis: win probabilities, team form breakdowns, match previews. That's how I make money. Your venue helps me attract fans; the AI analysis is what converts them to paying users. We both win when there are more engaged fans."

**"Can we think about it?"**
"Of course. I'm confirming venues this week because the first match is June 15. I'll follow up in two days — what's the best way to reach you?"
